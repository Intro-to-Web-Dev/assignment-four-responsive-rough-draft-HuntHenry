PINK FLOYD'S TOP 5 ALBUMS

ABOUT THE BAND

Pink Floyd is a psychedelic rock band founded in 1963 by university students Syd Barrett, Nick Mason, Roger Waters, and Richard Wright. They played small gigs and released singles up until 1967 when they released their debut album "The Piper at the Gates of Dawn" Which also featured new band member Roger Waters. Shortly thereafter in 1968 came the end of the Syd Barrett era of Pink Floyd. He had become heavily addicted to strong psychedelic drugs, mainly LSD, which caused him to be a liability to the band's performance. Barrett and Pink Floyd mutually agreed to part ways before the release of "Let There Be More Light". The band then released 5 albums between 1968 and 1972, one of which, "Meddle", is featured in this list. Then in 1973, they made their big break, releasing "The Dark Side of the Moon" This album was an instant classic and has been extremely popular throughout the years. A couple of years later, the band released "Wish You Were Here" which was an ode to former fallen bandmate Syd Barrett. The hit single off the album which is named the same is a melancholy song remembering Barrett. It is considered by many to be Pink Floyds best song. Next was "Animals" in 1977, a short album which drew similarities from people in society to animals. Then lastly, in 1979 Pink Floyd released "The Wall". This would be the last album of their glory days before the MTV era of music was ushered in. It was, like most of Pink Floyd's music, a shot at the system and became an anthem for many young people who want to escape from the control of school and government.
